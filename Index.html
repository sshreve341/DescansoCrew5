Type 2 Hand Crew(AD) Overview

A Type 2 On-Call (AD) Hand Crew is a wildland fire suppression crew made up of trained firefighters who are mobilized as needed to support incident operations. These crews provide critical ground-based firefighting support on initial attack and extended attack incidents.

  • Constructing and improving handline using hand tools (Pulaskis, shovels, McLeods, chainsaws
  • Cutting fireline in steep, rugged, or remote terrain where engines and heavy equipment cannot operate
  • Conducting mop-up and patrol to secure containment lines
  • Performing firing operations (when qualified and directed)
  • Supporting hose lays, structure protection, and engine crews
  • Assisting with fuels reduction and prescribed fire projects
  • Spike camp operations and extended assignments (often 14-day rolls)

Position Information

Currently seeking highly motivated individuals to fill our Type II Hand crew, Descanso Crew 5. This is an excellent opportunity to jumpstart a career in Wildland Fire with the U.S. Forest Service. We provide Basic and Refresher firefighter training.

  • Compensation: Students will be paid for all training (approx. 40 hrs) and field days, earning $22.16 - $24.36 per hour.
  • On-Call Status: This is an on-call firefighter position. Work is based on fire activity nationwide and local forest resource availability.
  • Commitment: When activated, members work 8-16 hour shifts with a 14-21 day commitment for local coverage or emergency assignments.
  • Physical Fitness: Crew members can expect to run or hike every day. You MUST pass the Work Capacity Test on Day 1 (3-mile walk with 45lb vest in 45 mins).
  • Logistics: Lodging and lunch are NOT provided. You are responsible for transportation, carpooling is recommended, and you must be on time every day.

Note: Once selected, you will be contacted via email by the Crew Boss.

Day 1 Requirements & Gear

Crew cohesion is important, and we need to look professional. Please adhere to the following:

  • Attire: Plain black t-shirt (NO advertising/logos), plain shorts/pants, and running type shoes.
  • Boots: APPROVED Wildland Firefighting Boots are REQUIRED on Day 1. Must have 8” upper leather with a non-skid/Vibram sole.
    Recommended: Boot World, Red Wing, Fire Etc., Carl’s Boots. Break them in before training!
  • Essentials: Black or brown belt, wristwatch, and knife.
  • Sustenance: Bring your own lunch and plenty of water. Transportation is your responsibility; carpooling is recommended.
